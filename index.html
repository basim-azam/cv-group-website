Welcome

The Computer Vision Group at The University of Melbourne conducts cutting-edge research in computer vision, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. Our work spans fundamental advances in deep learning to practical applications in medical imaging, remote sensing, and beyond.

Led by Dr. Naveed Akhtar, an ARC DECRA Fellow, our group focuses on developing interpretable and robust AI systems. We investigate adversarial machine learning, explainable AI, 3D point cloud analysis, and multi-modal learning.

Research Areas

Adversarial Machine Learning

Investigating vulnerabilities and defenses in deep neural networks against adversarial attacks.

Explainable AI

Developing interpretable models and understanding decision-making in deep learning systems.

3D Point Cloud Analysis

Processing and understanding 3D point cloud data for autonomous systems and robotics.

Medical Image Analysis

Applying deep learning to medical imaging for diagnosis and treatment planning.

Remote Sensing

Analyzing satellite and aerial imagery using advanced computer vision techniques.

Deep Learning for Vision

Advancing fundamental deep learning methods for visual recognition and understanding.
