News

January 2025

CVPR 2025 Paper Accepted

Our paper on plug-and-play interpretable responsible text-to-image generation has been accepted at CVPR 2025. This work presents a novel approach to controlling generative AI models through dual-space multi-facet concept control, enabling more responsible image generation.

September 2024

Two Papers Accepted at ECCV 2024

Our group has two papers accepted at ECCV 2024. The first paper investigates regulating model reliance on non-robust features, and the second explores adversarial robustness in vision transformers. Congratulations to all co-authors!

July 2024

New PhD Students Join the Group

We welcome Ali Alrasheed, Khawar Islam, Heethanjan Kanagalingam, Mohammed Ishaq Ansari, and Aryan Yazdan Parast to our research group as new PhD students. They will be working on topics including world models, multi-modal foundation models, and responsible generative AI.

March 2024

Dr. Akhtar Serving as Area Chair for CVPR 2024

Dr. Naveed Akhtar is serving as Area Chair for CVPR 2024, the premier conference in computer vision. This continues his active service to the research community.

January 2024

AAAI 2024 Paper on Point Cloud Processing

Our paper "Improved MLP Point Cloud Processing with High-Dimensional Positional Encoding" has been accepted at AAAI 2024. The work presents a novel approach to processing point cloud data using MLPs enhanced with positional encoding.

2023

Google Research Scholar Award

Dr. Akhtar received the Google Research Scholar Program Award to support research on adversarial robustness and interpretability of deep learning models.

2023

ACM Distinguished Speaker

Dr. Akhtar was recognized as an ACM Distinguished Speaker, acknowledging his contributions to the field of computer vision and machine learning through research and community engagement.

2021

WA's Early Career Scientist of the Year Finalist

Dr. Akhtar was a finalist for Western Australia's Early Career Scientist of the Year 2021, recognizing his contributions to adversarial machine learning and computer vision research.
