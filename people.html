Faculty

Dr. Naveed Akhtar

ARC DECRA Fellow, Senior Lecturer

School of Computing and Information Systems, The University of Melbourne

Dr. Akhtar is a computer vision and machine learning scientist with research interests in deep learning-based visual modeling. He is an ARC DECRA Fellow and has previously served at the University of Western Australia and Australian National University. His work has contributed to interpretability of deep learning models, adversarial attacks and defenses, point cloud analysis, medical image analysis, and hyperspectral image analysis.

Adversarial Machine Learning Explainable AI 3D Point Cloud Analysis Medical Image Analysis Remote Sensing Deep Learning for Vision

Honors: USERN Laureate in Formal Sciences, Google Research Scholar Program Award, ACM Distinguished Speaker, WA's Early Career Scientist of the Year 2021 Finalist

Service: Area Chair for CVPR, ECCV, WACV

Dr. Basim Azam

Research Fellow

School of Computing and Information Systems, The University of Melbourne

Dr. Azam is a researcher with focus on computer vision, machine learning, and explainable AI. Previously a Research Fellow at Griffith University, he investigated the explainability of AI algorithms, contributing to the development of more interpretable and effective AI systems. He completed his PhD at Griffith University, where his thesis on "Deep Learning based Context Adaptive Architectures for Image Parsing" earned him the Australian Research Council's Discovery Project Scholarship. His research encompasses developing techniques for object detection, image segmentation, remote sensing, and medical imaging applications.
