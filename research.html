Research

Our research spans fundamental advances in computer vision and machine learning to practical applications in healthcare, autonomous systems, and remote sensing. We focus on developing AI systems that are not only accurate but also interpretable, robust, and responsible.

Research Areas

Adversarial Machine Learning

Deep neural networks are vulnerable to adversarial examples - carefully crafted perturbations that can fool models while being imperceptible to humans. Our research investigates both the theoretical foundations and practical implications of adversarial vulnerabilities, developing robust defenses and understanding the security implications for deployed AI systems.

  • Adversarial attack generation and analysis
  • Certified and empirical defense mechanisms
  • Robustness evaluation and benchmarking
  • Physical-world adversarial attacks
  • Backdoor attacks on generative models

Explainable AI (XAI)

As AI systems are increasingly deployed in high-stakes applications, understanding their decision-making process becomes crucial. We develop methods to interpret deep learning models, creating explanations that are faithful to the model's actual reasoning while being comprehensible to human users.

  • Feature attribution and saliency methods
  • Concept-based explanations
  • Interpretable model architectures
  • Evaluation metrics for explanations
  • Human-AI interaction studies

3D Point Cloud Analysis

Point clouds from LiDAR and depth sensors are essential for autonomous vehicles, robotics, and augmented reality. We develop deep learning methods for understanding and processing 3D point cloud data, addressing challenges like irregularity, sparsity, and lack of structure.

  • Point cloud classification and segmentation
  • 3D object detection and tracking
  • Scene completion and reconstruction
  • Efficient point cloud processing
  • Robustness to sensor noise and corruption

Medical Image Analysis

We apply computer vision and deep learning to medical imaging problems, developing AI systems that can assist clinicians in diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient monitoring. Our work spans multiple imaging modalities and clinical applications.

  • Medical image segmentation
  • Disease classification and detection
  • Multi-modal medical imaging
  • Uncertainty quantification for clinical AI
  • Privacy-preserving medical AI

Remote Sensing

Satellite and aerial imagery provide critical data for environmental monitoring, urban planning, and disaster response. We develop computer vision methods tailored to the unique challenges of remote sensing data, including large scale, multiple spectral bands, and temporal sequences.

  • Hyperspectral image analysis
  • Land cover classification
  • Change detection and monitoring
  • Small target detection in infrared imagery
  • Super-resolution for satellite imagery

Deep Learning for Vision

We advance fundamental deep learning methods for visual recognition and understanding. This includes developing new architectures, training strategies, and theoretical understanding of deep networks applied to vision tasks.

  • Vision transformers and attention mechanisms
  • Multi-modal learning (vision-language models)
  • Self-supervised and unsupervised learning
  • Efficient neural network design
  • Foundation models for vision

Responsible Generative AI

Generative AI models like text-to-image systems raise important questions about safety, bias, and misuse. We research methods to make generative AI more responsible, including content moderation, bias mitigation, and interpretable generation controls.

  • Safe text-to-image generation
  • Bias detection and mitigation
  • Interpretable generation controls
  • Deepfake detection
  • AI-generated content attribution

Funding & Collaborations

Our research is supported by the Australian Research Council (ARC), including DECRA fellowships and Discovery Projects. We collaborate with researchers at leading institutions worldwide and maintain partnerships with industry for translational research.

Key collaborators include researchers at the University of Western Australia, Australian National University, University of Central Florida, and various international institutions.
